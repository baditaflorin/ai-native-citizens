# Code of Conduct

AI-Native Citizens uses the Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct, version 2.1, adapted for civic build sprints.

Full upstream text: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project and its local events open to people regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that build a useful, direct, and welcoming civic technical community.

## Expected Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and accept constructive feedback.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the public problem being worked on.
- Show empathy toward other participants.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Taking credit for work done by others.
- Pressuring teams to give up ownership of their work.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a civic public setting.

## Sprint-Specific Rules

Projects belong to teams. Administrations, sponsors, mentors, judges, and organizers do not take team work without explicit consent.

No sprint prototype may be presented as production-ready public software unless the team explicitly says so and has completed the necessary legal, security, privacy, and operational checks.

Participants must receive education, visibility, feedback, network access, and real ownership. A civic sprint is not unpaid procurement.

## Enforcement

Local organizers are responsible for enforcing this code at local events. Official Local Sprints must name at least two conduct contacts before participant registration opens.

Possible responses include a warning, facilitated conversation, removal from a session, removal from the event, rejection of a project from demo day, or loss of official sprint status.

Reports can be sent to the local event conduct contacts or opened privately with the steward team through the repository owner.
