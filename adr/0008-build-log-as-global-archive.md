# ADR-0008: Treat the Build Log as the Strategic Asset

## Context

Many civic hackathons produce demos and then lose the learning. AI-Native Citizens needs a way to compare events, understand how teams actually use AI, and build a public record of successes, errors, corrections, and follow-through.

## Decision

Every team must publish an AI Build Log. The log records the problem, named user, tools, five key prompts, what AI generated well, what humans corrected, what was hard, what works, what does not work, next steps, and repo/demo links. The judging rubric gives 30 points to the AI-native build process.

## Consequences

The archive becomes more valuable with every city. After 20 events, the project has evidence about real civic AI practice rather than opinions about AI. Teams must spend time documenting, and organizers must enforce the requirement. This burden is accepted because the archive is the asymmetric strategic advantage.

## Alternatives Considered

Only publishing winners was rejected because it hides learning. Optional build logs were rejected because optional process artifacts disappear under time pressure. Video-only demos were rejected because they are harder to search, compare, and verify.
