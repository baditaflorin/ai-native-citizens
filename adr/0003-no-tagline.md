# ADR-0003: Do Not Use a Tagline

## Context

Early movement brands often add a slogan to explain themselves. This project already has a strong name, a clear method, and a public manifesto voice. A tagline would invite generic civic-tech phrasing and dilute the name.

## Decision

AI-Native Citizens has no tagline. The action phrase is **Become an AI-Native Citizen**. Public copy may use descriptive subtitles such as "Humans building public AI in their cities" in a local context, but no phrase becomes a permanent tagline.

## Consequences

The brand stays sharper and less corporate. Local chapters cannot invent their own official slogans. The name must carry the work. Writers should explain the format in plain sentences instead of relying on a slogan.

## Alternatives Considered

"Civic AI through building" was descriptive but flat. "Build public AI locally" was useful as copy but not as a brand layer. More emotional slogans were rejected because they would age quickly and push the project toward campaign language instead of durable movement language.
