# ADR-0005: Scale Through a Burning Man-Style Local Chapter Hierarchy

## Context

AI-Native Citizens must scale across cities without turning every local event into a central production. The movement needs open replication, quality control for official status, and a path for larger institutions to participate without absorbing the identity.

## Decision

The chapter model has three tiers. Community Edition is open to anyone under basic conditions. Official Local Sprint requires approval, verified lead, minimum mentors and challenges, public demo, build logs, final report, and correct brand use. Certified City Partner adds organizer training, custom challenge design support, sponsor deck support, optional facilitator, global listing, and Summit eligibility.

## Consequences

Local energy can move without waiting for permission, while the official brand keeps a quality bar. Municipalities, universities, and larger organizations have a clear path that does not make them owners of the movement. The central steward team must maintain the rules and say no when needed.

## Alternatives Considered

A fully centralized model would bottleneck the movement. A fully open model would make quality, safety, and brand meaning impossible to defend. A franchise model was rejected because it would over-commercialize the first phase.
