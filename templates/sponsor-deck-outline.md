# Sponsor Deck Outline

Use this as a practical sponsor deck skeleton. Keep it short. Sponsors should understand the civic value, what they fund, and what they do not own.

## 1. Title

AI-Native Citizens: [City] ([dates])

## 2. The Problem

Cities are facing AI from the outside: vendors, consultants, and policy panic. Local communities need practical AI literacy through building.

## 3. The Format

48 to 72 hour civic AI sprint. Real public problems. Mixed teams. Mentors. Global rubric. Mandatory build logs. Public demo.

## 4. Why It Matters

Participants learn by building. Cities get working prototypes and public evidence. Sponsors support practical civic capacity instead of another panel.

## 5. What Sponsors Fund

- Venue costs if not donated.
- Food and drinks.
- Mentor travel.
- Prize pool or micro-grants.
- Translation.
- Documentation and video.
- Post-event continuation.

## 6. Sponsor Benefits

- Logo on event materials.
- Mention in opening and closing.
- Demo-day presence.
- Public thank-you in report.
- Optional challenge category if approved by organizers.

## 7. Sponsor Boundaries

Sponsors do not own team work. Sponsors do not control judging. Sponsors do not turn the sprint into recruitment theater. Sponsors do not receive private participant data.
