# Participant Guide

Welcome to AI-Native Citizens: [City].

You are here to build something useful for public life, learn how to use AI-native workflows, publish your process, and demo honestly.

## What You Will Build

Each team chooses or adapts a civic challenge, names a specific user, builds a 48 to 72 hour prototype, and publishes an AI build log.

## Required Outputs

- Working prototype or inspectable artifact.
- 5-minute demo path.
- Repository or shareable project link.
- AI build log using `templates/ai-build-log.md`.
- Clear statement of what works and what does not.

## Team Ownership

Projects belong to teams.

Open-source is the default, with clear license.

No prototype is promised as production-ready software.

Administrations do not take team work without explicit consent.

Continuation requires explicit acknowledgment, credit, micro-grant, or contract.

You should leave with education, visibility, feedback, network, and real ownership.

## How To Use AI In The Sprint

Use AI for research, framing, design, code, debugging, testing, writing, and demo preparation. Do not paste sensitive private data. Do not trust factual claims without sources. Keep prompts and corrections in the build log.

## Demo Rules

You have 5 minutes. Show the live path. Name the user. State limits. Do not hide broken parts.

## Safety Rules

Do not create tools that expose private data, impersonate public institutions, give legal certainty without review, or encourage users to rely on unverified claims.

## Where To Start

1. Pick a challenge.
2. Name the user.
3. Write the smallest useful demo path.
4. Generate a rough brief with AI.
5. Red-team it.
6. Build one path.
7. Verify sources.
8. Publish the build log.
