# Challenge Bank

These starter challenges are designed for 48 to 72 hour civic AI sprints. Local organizers should adapt names, data sources, and user examples to the city.

## CH-01: Local Budget Explorer

Problem: City budgets are public but hard for residents to read.

User: Ana, a parent who wants to know why her neighborhood playground has no repairs.

Why it matters: Public money becomes less accountable when the documents are unreadable.

Possible data sources: city budget PDFs, open-data portals, council budget annexes, procurement datasets.

48h MVP scope: upload or link budget files, extract categories, show plain-language summaries, search by neighborhood or theme.

Difficulty: Medium.

Useful AI workflows: WF-06, WF-10, WF-12.

Demo examples: ask "How much is planned for parks?" and show source-linked answer.

Continuation path: publish cleaned tables, add year comparison, recruit finance mentor.

## CH-02: Public Services Navigator

Problem: Residents cannot easily find the right office, form, fee, and next step for basic services.

User: Mihai, a new resident trying to register an address change.

Why it matters: Confusing service paths waste time and punish people with less administrative knowledge.

Possible data sources: municipality service pages, PDFs, phone lists, office hours, local FAQ pages.

48h MVP scope: searchable guide with step-by-step answers and source citations.

Difficulty: Easy.

Useful AI workflows: WF-01, WF-02, WF-10.

Demo examples: type a service need and get required documents, office, fee, and caveats.

Continuation path: validate top 20 services with municipal staff.

## CH-03: FOIA Request Generator

Problem: Citizens often do not know how to ask for public information in a legally useful way.

User: Elena, a local resident asking for contract documents about road repairs.

Why it matters: Public information rights are weak if only specialists can use them.

Possible data sources: FOIA law text, city contact pages, prior request examples, NGO guides.

48h MVP scope: guided form that produces a precise request, subject line, and delivery checklist.

Difficulty: Easy.

Useful AI workflows: WF-01, WF-09, WF-10.

Demo examples: generate a request for procurement documents and show legal basis.

Continuation path: add jurisdiction packs and response tracker.

## CH-04: Council Decisions Explainer

Problem: Council decisions are published in dense documents that residents rarely understand in time to react.

User: Radu, a neighborhood association member tracking zoning decisions.

Why it matters: Local democracy is weaker when decisions are technically public but practically hidden.

Possible data sources: council agenda PDFs, decision archives, meeting minutes, zoning maps.

48h MVP scope: extract recent decisions, summarize what changed, who is affected, and where to verify.

Difficulty: Medium.

Useful AI workflows: WF-06, WF-10, WF-11.

Demo examples: paste a decision PDF and produce a resident-facing brief.

Continuation path: subscription alerts by topic or neighborhood.

## CH-05: Public Procurement Watcher

Problem: Procurement notices and awards are scattered, technical, and hard to watch over time.

User: Ioana, a local journalist following repeated awards to the same supplier.

Why it matters: Procurement is where public money becomes real power.

Possible data sources: national procurement portals, city contracts, budget annexes, company registries.

48h MVP scope: track a small category, summarize new awards, flag repeat suppliers or unusual changes.

Difficulty: Hard.

Useful AI workflows: WF-06, WF-10, WF-03.

Demo examples: show supplier timeline for one procurement category.

Continuation path: add human review workflow and publish methodology.

## CH-06: Civic Issue Map

Why it matters: Patterns stay invisible when complaints remain isolated.

Possible data sources: public reports, open maps, photos submitted by participants, existing issue lists.

48h MVP scope: map issues, cluster by type, generate status notes, export CSV.

Difficulty: Medium.

Useful AI workflows: WF-01, WF-02, WF-11.

Demo examples: add five reports and show clustered neighborhood problems.

## CH-07: School Rights Assistant

Why it matters: Rights that cannot be understood are rights that cannot be used.

Possible data sources: education law, school regulations, inspectorate pages, NGO explainers.

48h MVP scope: question-and-answer guide with source links, plain-language caveats, and escalation paths.

Difficulty: Medium.

## CH-08: NGO Admin Copilot

Problem: Small NGOs lose time on repetitive reporting, grant, and volunteer admin.

User: Cristina, an NGO coordinator preparing a funder report after work hours.

Why it matters: Civic groups burn out when admin eats the time meant for public work.

Possible data sources: anonymized reports, grant templates, activity logs, public annual reports.

48h MVP scope: generate report drafts from structured notes, checklist missing info, export Markdown.

Difficulty: Easy.

Useful AI workflows: WF-01, WF-09, WF-10.

## CH-09: Local Journalism Research Assistant

Problem: Local reporters need fast help reading documents, timelines, and public records.

Why it matters: Local journalism has too much document work and too little time.

Possible data sources: council minutes, procurement portals, court portals, public statements, archives.

Difficulty: Hard.

Useful AI workflows: WF-06, WF-10, WF-03.

Demo examples: upload three documents and generate a timeline with source links.

Continuation path: newsroom pilot with strict verification rules.

## CH-10: PDF-to-Open-Data Converter

Problem: Public information is often trapped in scanned or messy PDFs.

User: Daria, a civic data volunteer turning permit lists into reusable tables.

Why it matters: Open data does not exist when the only source is a locked PDF.

Possible data sources: local PDFs, scanned tables, annual reports, annexes, meeting packs.

48h MVP scope: upload PDF, extract tables, let humans correct fields, export CSV and JSON.

Difficulty: Medium.

Useful AI workflows: WF-06, WF-10, WF-12.

Demo examples: convert a council annex into a cleaned table with correction log.

Continuation path: publish conversion recipes and validation tests.
