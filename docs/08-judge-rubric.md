# Judge Rubric

This is the global standard. Local events must use this rubric verbatim. Global comparability is part of the strategic value of the build-log archive.

| Criterion | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| AI-native build process | 30 |
| Working prototype | 25 |
| Civic usefulness | 20 |
| UX and clarity | 15 |
| Continuation potential | 10 |

Maximum score: 100.

## AI-Native Build Process: 30 Points

Excellent (27-30): The team used AI across research, framing, UX, coding, testing, demo, and documentation; kept strong human review; published specific prompts; corrected model errors; and made verification visible.

Good (21-26): The team used AI in several meaningful parts of the process and documented prompts and corrections, but verification or workflow breadth is uneven.

Fair (15-20): The team used AI mainly for code or copy generation, with limited build-log detail and weak evidence of correction.

## Working Prototype: 25 Points

Excellent (23-25): The prototype runs live, completes the main user path, handles at least one failure state, and has clear source or data boundaries.

Good (18-22): The prototype runs and shows the core idea, but has fragile edges, manual steps, or limited error handling.

Fair (13-17): The prototype is partly clickable or partly functional, but the main path depends on explanation.

## Civic Usefulness: 20 Points

Excellent (18-20): The project addresses a real public problem for a named user, with clear stakes and plausible local use.

Good (14-17): The problem is civic and useful, but the user or adoption path needs sharper definition.

Fair (10-13): The civic relevance is visible, but the project is too broad, generic, or detached from a real user.

## UX and Clarity: 15 Points

Excellent (14-15): The interface is understandable, accessible, plain-language, and built around the user's task.

Good (11-13): The interface is usable but has some confusing terms, missing states, or layout issues.

Fair (8-10): The interface needs guidance from the team to make sense.

## Continuation Potential: 10 Points

Excellent (9-10): The next 30 days are concrete: owner, repo, license, data needs, partner ask, and first maintenance steps.

Good (7-8): The team has a plausible next step but lacks one or two details.

Fair (5-6): The team wants to continue but has no clear owner, ask, or maintenance plan.

## Judge Notes

Judges score prototypes, process, and honesty. Do not reward claims that are not backed by a working demo or build log. Do not punish a team for naming limits clearly.
