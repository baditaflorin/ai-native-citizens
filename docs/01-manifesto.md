# Manifesto

AI-Native Citizens are humans. Citizens who refuse to be passive consumers of AI built by others.

Every city has problems people complain about for years. A form that makes no sense. A budget nobody can read. A council decision buried in a PDF. A public service that works only if you already know the hallway, the office, and the person behind the desk. These problems used to sit in the gap between anger and capacity. Now ordinary citizens can turn a complaint into a working prototype in a weekend.

The enemy is not AI. The enemy is capture. Corporate capture that turns public data into private advantage. Regulatory capture that lets the slowest institutions define the future for everyone else. Extractive AI that learns from public life and gives nothing public back. A city that only buys AI will be shaped by vendors. A city that learns to build with AI can argue back with working software.

The method is simple and hard. Bring people together for 48 to 72 hours. Start with real public problems. Use AI-native workflows across research, framing, design, coding, testing, and documentation. Build something that works enough to be judged by users, not slogans. Keep a mandatory build log: the prompts, the wrong turns, the parts AI got right, the parts humans corrected, the tests that failed, the demo that survived. End in public, with working prototypes and clear limits.

This work belongs in cities, neighborhoods, schools, NGOs, newsrooms, universities, libraries, and mayor's offices that want to serve people instead of being sold another dashboard. It belongs with students who should not wait four years to make something real. It belongs with journalists tracing a contract trail. It belongs with parents trying to understand school rights. It belongs with municipal staff who know the broken process better than any consultant.

We come from a longer line. Digital natives learned the network by living inside it. Netizens treated the internet as a public place, not only a market. Cypherpunks wrote code because speeches were not enough. AI-Native Citizens inherit that energy with this generation's tools. We do not worship the tools. We use them in public, against public problems, with public receipts.

There are three doors in. Become an AI-Native Citizen: join a team, build a prototype, publish the log. Host one: bring a sprint to your city and make the work visible. Fund one: pay for rooms, food, mentors, prizes, micro-grants, translation, and the next thirty days when most civic hackathons die.

We are not waiting for permission to understand the systems that shape our lives.

We are building in public.
